Pathways to Programming: A Dev Bootcamp Interview Series

Posted on 30 Nov 2012 by Scott James

Tweet

Spend some time at Dev Bootcamp and you'll see somebody walking around with a t-shirt that reads "Coding is the new literacy." We believe that, and it's a core reason why we do what we do. We also believe that there are as many paths to programming as there are programmers, and that coding without college is possible. With the right mix of mindset, motivation, and support, anyone can learn to code.

In the Pathways to Programming interview series we talk to people who took the leap from non-technical to professional coder and explore questions like:

How do people learn to code?

What do people do with their new skills?

Here are a few interview excerpts:

“You have to realize that you can’t know everything about everything, and be okay with that.” -- Adam Wills on The Importance Of WHY When Learning To Program

“Learning to program is no different than learning to cook or learning carpentry. It’s all about continuous practice over a lot of years.” -- Breck Yunits on Taking The Red Pill And Learning To Program

“Don’t be afraid to fail. On the contrary, fail often, fail fast; that’s the only way to learn.” -- Dragos Roua on Curiosity And Stubbornness: One Digital Nomad's Advice On Learning To Code

“There are few things sweeter than cracking open a beer and some crappy code then refactoring it into something simple and expressive.” -- Zee Spencer on Legos, Pizza, And Passion: Learning To Code From A To Zee

“The more you learn, you more you understand about how little you know.” -– Jeremy Lewis on How One Man Went From Lindy Hopper to Senior Software Engineer

“The BEST way to get into programming is to hang out with great programmers and just do it.” -– Andrew Kelleher on How a Line Cook Learned to Track Rockets and Became a Senior Developer

“You often have to let go of your assumptions about the problem in order to solve it.” -– Luca Pellicoro on The Importance of Ongoing Learning as a Programmer

“Having a solid path to learning to code and a community to get feedback from would have saved me countless hours of frustration and forum searches.” -– John McAlester on How Community Helps Individuals Learn to Code

“I am challenged every day, which I have never found in a job before now. This makes me much happier.” -– Jeffrey Baird on Finding Happiness in his Transition from Founder and Product person to Coder

“I actually think there are very few ‘mistakes’ at all, just a broken mindset.” -- Sahil Lavingia on Solving Your Own Problems and Learning To Program

“In the world we live in now, knowing how to build makes you unstoppable.” -- Zach Shapiro on Transitioning From Product Person To Coder

”I’ve been programming for a little over a year now and it’s been one of the best years of my life because of it.” -- Phil Aquilina on Starting A New Job After Dev Bootcamp

“Surround yourself with people who are going to challenge and stretch you, but also won’t make you feel bad for asking questions.” -- Malcolm Newsome on The Art Of Learning

“Inexperience does NOT mean inadequate.” -– Kim Lanning on her Transition From Teacher To Coder

Have your own story? We'd love to hear from you.

If you've taken your own unconventional path from "nogrammer to programmer," we want to hear from you. Send me a short email at scott.james@devbootcamp.com and let's talk.
