Interview: How Sahil Lavingia, Founder of Gumroad, Learned to Program

Posted on 11 Dec 2012 by Scott James

Tweet

"I actually think there are very few 'mistakes' at all, just a broken mindset."

- Sahil Lavingia

This week on our Pathways to Programming series we're excited to talk with Sahil Lavingia, a founding designer at Pinterest, who learned to code before founding Gumroad, a tool that helps "democratize the ability to sell stuff online."

Lavingia talks about deciding to learn how to "solve my own problems and all that" and how that plays into building Gumroad. Learning coding and improving the way we sell online? We think that's pretty awesome.

Who are you? Tell us about yourself.

Hi! My name's Sahil. I work on this thing called Gumroad where I spend most of my time trying to figure out how to make our product better and better for our customers.

What were you doing before? Why did you decide to code?

I used to work at Pinterest. Before then, I was in school — at USC in LA.

How did you learn? What resources were most valuable?

I googled and stack overflowed a lot. I decided I wanted to learn how to build cool things for the web. Solve my own problems and all that. Turns out, the Internet had most of the answers I sought — so I just got good at finding them.

What were some of the biggest mistakes you made? What did you learn from them?

No clue. My brain (our brains) do a good job of forgetting that. Plus, I think everything has worked out super well so far so anything that may have looked like a mistake turned out to not be one at all. I actually think there are very few "mistakes" at all, just a broken mindset.

What were some of the biggest obstacles you found outside the actual learning?

Burn out. I never thought that spending a lot of time doing what you leads to you not wanting to do that anymore!

What have you been doing with it?

Trying to build impactful things for lots of people. Maybe helping others do the same, too.

How do you find your life different emotionally speaking?

Deep! I think about things a lot more logically and procedurally now. In general I think knowing about a lot of the structural pieces of logic and how that works is super important. It turns out that coding is a good way to learn that (though there are others).

What’s next for you, and where are you headed?

I want to work on Gumroad for a long, long time. Make it really awesome and world-changing for many, many millions of people.

Any thoughts for others who are thinking about or starting to learn to code?

What are you waiting for?
