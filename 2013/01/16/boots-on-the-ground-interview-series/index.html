Boots on the Ground Interview Series

Posted on 16 Jan 2013 by Scott James

Tweet

In our "Boots on the Ground" series we'll be inviting some of the "boots" who are currently students at Dev Bootcamp to share some of their personal story.

Dev Bootcamp students share a common love for coding, but how each "boot" came to that love, what each of them wants to do with it, and what excites them about the journey is always a unique story.

Here are some of those stories:

“Coding will make you a better entrepreneur, banker, hedge fund manager or whatever profession you ultimately choose." -- Allen Lee on Why Coding Will Make You Better At Whatever You Ultimately Do

“I go home completely drained and all I can think about is… I can’t wait for tomorrow.” -- Alex Ray on being Completely Drained And Ready For Tomorrow

“DBC is the most challenging and rewarding learning experience I’ve ever had.” -– Feifan Wang on [Something Great and Beautiful: The Transition From Sales To Coding] (http://devbootcamp.com/2013/04/11/transitioning-from-sales-to-coding-to-build-something-great-and-beautiful-feifan-wang-interview/)

“Dev Bootcamp is where curiosity meets utility, and I’ve discovered that’s where I learn best.” -– David Wen on Learning At The Intersection Of Curiosity And Utility

“The beautiful thing about being with 15 other people that are trying to change their lives is that you can’t help but live as your fullest self.” -– Christopher Sell on Coding A Path To Your Fullest Self

“After going to more than 10 years of formal education (med school, medical specialty, business school), this is by far my best learning experience.” -– Omar Rayward on Changing The World From Your Keyboard: Why An Opthalmologist Is Learning To Code

“That grabbed me and I thought, I have to be a part of that.” -– Justin Silverman on his Journey From Private Equity To Coding

"I was basically instantly in love with coding because not only was I doing much the same work as I did in my academic life, I was actually creating something at the end of the day." -- Eno Compton on Not Letting School Get In The Way Of His Education
