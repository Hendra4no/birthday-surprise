Completely Drained And Ready For Tomorrow

Posted on 16 Apr 2013 by Scott James

Tweet

"I go home completely drained and all I can think about is... I can't wait for tomorrow."

-- Alex Ray

Dev Bootcamp students share a common love for coding, but how each individual student came to that love is always a unique story. In our Boots on the Ground series we invite the "boots" who are currently at Dev Bootcamp to share some of their personal story.

Today we hear from Alex Ray, who is making the transition from bartender to programmer. He talks about challenging assumptions, why learning to code felt inevitable for him, and why he is "learning more than ever before all while having more fun than I ever thought possible."

Quick Facts

Age: 21

Hometown: Salt Lake City, Utah

Most recent work: Bartender

Tell us about you. How do you describe yourself?

I am very passionate about life and what I choose to do in it. I hold a strong belief that you should always challenge assumptions, live every day like its your last, and enjoy every second along the way; the bad and good.

Why are you switching careers and why did you choose Dev Bootcamp?

Not only is this a natural transition for me but if feels like it was inevitable.

When I wasn't bartending I was always on my computer, creating, learning or playing with it. I found programming empowering, challenging and extremely rewarding. 

I choose Dev Bootcamp because they encompass everything that I truly believe in, this place is right in so many ways. Anyone who has been here knows the life-changing effect it has on you first hand. 

How is your transition going?

It's going really well. I'm consistently being pushed to my limits, learning more than ever before all while having more fun than I ever thought possible.

Where you coding before you got here?

Yes, I was. I made three or four websites and one Tumblr theme before I arrived at Dev Bootcamp.

What do you do when you're not coding?

I like to seek out and pursue experiences, whether that be exploring a farm in the country side, playing pool at an english-style pub, or sailing across the bay.

Every new experience gives me a little more of life that I never knew before. 

How is your experience at Dev Bootcamp going?

Absolutely amazing and nothing less than life changing. All the pressure, understanding, love and support is evolving me into a better person inside and out.

I go home completely drained and all I can think about is.... I can't wait for tomorrow. 

Any closing thoughts for someone considering applying?

Yes, be sure this is what you want, as life changing and amazing as it is, it's not for everyone. Make sure your are truly committed to doing this before you dive in. 

That being said, if this is for you and you are willing to put in everything you got, then this may be the best decision you will ever make. 

Remember luck is being prepared when opportunity strikes. The opportunity is in front of you, the only question is are you prepared?

This post is a part of the "Boots On The Ground" series which tells the stories of Dev Bootcamp students - where they came from, where they're going, and what excites them about the journey.
