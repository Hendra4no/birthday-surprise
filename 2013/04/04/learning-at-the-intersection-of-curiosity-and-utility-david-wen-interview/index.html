Learning At The Intersection Of Curiosity And Utility

Posted on 04 Apr 2013 by Scott James

Tweet

"Dev Bootcamp is where curiosity meets utility, and I've discovered that's where I learn best."

-- David Wen

Dev Bootcamp students share a common love for coding, but how each individual student came to that love is always a unique story. In our Boots on the Ground series we invite the "boots" who are currently at Dev Bootcamp to share some of their personal story.

Today we hear from David Wen, who is making the transition to coding from management consulting. He talks about learning about himself and what appears in a browser at the same time, his drive to build up his technical skills, and why he enjoys the struggle of learning to code. As he says on his personal site JustSayWen.com, "Some say life is not about finding yourself; rather, it’s about creating yourself."

Quick Facts

Age: 25

Hometown: Born in Taipei, Taiwan. Grew up in White Rock, Canada

Most recent work: Senior Consultant at SECOR Management Consulting (now KPMG)

Tell us about you. How do you describe yourself?

Curious kid. I like to play and learn, ideally simultaneously. I love to travel, take pictures, play sports, and I generally feel very lucky to be alive and healthy.

I'm an entrepreneur of not just business ideas, but philanthropic ones too. If I could do more than one career I would also be a storywriter or work at NASA.

Why are you switching careers and why did you choose Dev Bootcamp?

Always wanted to get into the startup world and I have spent the past few years building up my business credentials working. I've done work ranging from my own small start-ups and not-for-profits to billion-dollar mergers and leading client teams at multi-national corporations.

I want to now fill in my technical skills and heard that Dev Bootcamp was the best place to do it. I decided to move from Vancouver to San Fran to take the course about an hour after I heard about it.

How is your transition going?

Well, I don't really realize I'm in San Francisco since I spend almost all my waking hours inside. That said, on the odd times I see California in the day time I love it. The weather is consistently pleasant and the people are extraordinarily nice. And that's coming from a Canadian!

Were you coding before you got here?

I made some sites using Wordpress and helped many friends with their sites if they were simple enough.

I never got to a level where I could create sophisticated apps though, which really bugged me. 

I did some VBA in Excel for work before I got here, and the little that I did there seemed to blow everyone away since they didn't want to go anywhere near code.

What do you do when you’re not coding?

Read, play my ukulele, play sports, travel, take photos, write, see friends, etc.

How is your experience at Dev Bootcamp going?

Dev Bootcamp is where curiosity meets utility, and I've discovered that's where I learn best.

It's such a great feeling to be able to have fun every day while knowing it is contributing positively to your desired future career path. 

I love problem solving and there's no greater place to be than the coding world for that. Especially when your code causes problems all the time :) I've met amazing people that I would be proud to call my friends and I've learned as much about myself as I have about how to make things appear in a browser.

Any closing thoughts for someone considering applying?

Don't do it. Just kidding. Absolutely do it. Keep in mind it's called Dev Bootcamp for a reason. You'll enjoy the journey if you enjoy the struggle and you'll enjoy the outcome if you come in knowing what you want to get out of the program. Good luck!

This post is a part of the "Boots On The Ground" series which tells the stories of Dev Bootcamp students - where they came from, where they're going, and what excites them about the journey.
