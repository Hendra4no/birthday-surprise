Announcing Dev Bootcamp New York City

Posted on 30 Oct 2013 by Tanner Welsh

Tweet

Every 3 weeks, the doors of Dev Bootcamp open for 20 determined students to begin nine weeks of intensive training. This ritual has occurred dozens of times before at our Chicago and San Francisco locations, each with a different group of wide-eyed initiates. Today, we are excited to announce that another city is joining the DBC family. We are coming to New York City in 2014. As of today, applications for our inaugural cohort are now open.

On March 31 of next year, our doors will open to 20 new students. I will be there to welcome them with my other co-founder, Lloyd Nimetz, and the founders of our SF and Chicago branches, Shereef Bishay and Dave Hoover. I know I speak for all of us in saying that we will surely be wearing the biggest goofy smiles, simultaneously overjoyed and nervous. All of our efforts are geared towards that day: we aim to build another unparalleled training program for aspiring web developers. And we are so freakin' excited that we get to build it in New York City.

Why we <3 NYC

For one, New York has a thriving tech community with its own rich history, stretching from the pioneering work of the Interactive Telecommunications Program to current ground-breaking initiatives such as those of the Computer Science Foundation of New York City (CSNYC). All signs point to even more evolution and growth, with strong support from both the private and public sectors.

We are thrilled to join the ranks of other great coding schools like The Flatiron School, Hacker School, and General Assembly. They have paved the way for the particular bootcamp-style programs here in the city, and they continue to provide quality education for aspiring programmers.

The wonderful collection of innovative companies founded and operating in New York City is another big plus. From Etsy to MongoDB, FourSquare to MakerBot, Tumblr to Kickstarter, and so many others, it is inspiring to be so close to such a wealth of creative enterprise.

There are many, many other reasons that we love being in New York City, too many to list here. The city is vibrant and bursting with potential, full of creative people and innovative ideas about education, technology, and the myriad ways that they intersect. Buy me a beer sometime and I'd be happy to share more.

An ambitious vision

I hope that I have adequately expressed just how excited I am. Maybe you believe me, maybe you think I'm just trying to sell something. Either way, there is one last thing that I would like to share. Take from it what you will, but please don't judge too harshly. Because I'm about to talk about something that is very important to me, and I'm going to get a little sentimental.

I'm going to talk about education.

Education is, for me, a reason for living. By education I don't mean school, or study, or grades, or accreditation. The kind of education that I care about is the kind that transforms lives; it is the kind of education that makes you feel more connected to the world around you than you ever thought possible; the kind of education that pulls you beyond the limits of your knowledge and leaves you standing breathless and in awe of the truths you never knew existed. This is the kind of education that I strive to build and to make available to as many people as possible.

The reason that I'm sharing this is because building a school like ours takes a metric ton of work and success is not guaranteed. Thankfully the challenge and the effort is a ton of fun in its own right. But it would be ultimately impossible (not to mention futile) without the shared dedication of our team.

With this ethos as our guide, we've built some pretty great schools in San Francisco and Chicago, and they keep getting better. Now we look forward to a new challenge in a new city, and we can think of no better place to be than New York City.


If you share this vision (or you're just curious to hear how crazy I really am), email me at tanner@devbootcamp.com. Don't be scared, I'm actually a pretty mild-mannered guy in real life. We are also currently looking for some awesome teachers to come play with us, so if that sounds like something you'd be into, check out our jobs page.
