What Dev Bootcamp Graduates Are Building

Posted on 07 Oct 2013 by Staff

Tweet

Last week we wrote about Jennifer Dewalt, who undertook the incredible "learn by doing" project of building 180 apps in 180 days. Last Friday we were proud to celebrate with our graduates who will take what they have learned at Dev Bootcamp and make amazing things happen out in the world.

It's amazing what builders can do when we put our minds to it and make it happen.

Since we can't invite everyone to our amazing our internal demo day, here's an inside look at what they have been building:

Greenlight

A dating service that help you find singles in your immediate vicinity, in real-time. When you allow our geolocation services on the app, you'll immediately be redirected to other singles just a short distance away. You have a choice: you can give them the greenlight, or pump the brakes! If someone you've "green lighted" hits you back, you're notified, and then have the option to meet up immediately.

Team: Richie Serna, Ali Tehrani, Usman Chaudhary, Sam Carow

.

SiteKick

SiteKick helps bloggers take their digital publishing skills to the next level. After logging in with a Tumblr account, a user can use siteKick to examine the structure and content of his or her most recent posts. The app gives each post a score and suggests improvements based on SEO best practices.

Team: Luisa Emmi Beck, Alex Fiore, Gabriela Zamudio, and Dan Apczynski

.

OpenNGO

OpenNGO supports the open-information initiative of HelpArgentina, an Argentine charity working to strengthen Argentina’s social sector. This website will provide donors and other socially-minded citizens unprecedented access to data on Argentine non-profits. Users can search for organizations and read about their programs, teams, financials and more. The site’s clean layout, solr-powered search, developer API and .xls download features all enhance the organization’s mission of increased NGO transparency.

Team: Lloyd Nimetz, Doktor Gurson, Lily Jarusvasunt, Alejandro Barbero

.

Jailbird

Jailbird means you only have to remember one phone number. Whether you've lost your phone or it's out of juice, find the nearest phone and access the numbers you need the most. Call a single person or send your customizable groups an SMS or voice blast. Great in a pinch or for sharing great news with friends and family quickly.

Team: Alex Flores, Nick Saldivar, Elaine Tsai, Charlie Young

.

NewsShark

NewsShark delivers news articles on topics important to you. You can create "channels" to listen for relevant stories, and you rate the ones that you like or dislike. Over time, your channel learns about your preferences and delivers customized recommendations based on article characteristics such as author, publication, word-count, keywords, readability metrics, and more. Utilizing our own in-house machine learning system and content-ranking algorithm, NewsShark does for the news what Pandora does for music: we filter out the noise.

Team: Ian Root, George Pradhan, Christian-Robert Joseph, Thomas Landon

.

HuemorMe

HeumorMe is designed to control your home's Philips Hue LED light bulbs from anywhere in the world. Monitor your light-bridge with the DeamonInsideMe program running on your Raspberry Pi, or virtually any computer.

Team: Chae O'Keefe, Steven Swigart, Jimmy Wong, Rhonda Jezek
