What The World Looks Like After 180 Days Of Websites

Posted on 03 Oct 2013 by Staff

Tweet

A few months ago, we posted about Jennifer Dewalt. Jennifer was a new coder who had chosen to learn by challenging herself to create a new web application every single day for 180 days - six full months.

She knew she wanted to learn by doing. She knew she wanted to accelerate her way through the learning process. She knew she wanted to pull in the ideas and expertise of others.

Her strategy was one which kept her on the edge of her comfort zone for a significant period of time, but which created a momentum and, it seems, a community of interest and support that many others would have loved to have when learning to code - or really, learning anything.

Her interest came from her background in fine art. Dewalt wrote that, in effect, her interest in art was about how people communicate, and she wanted to be literate in the communication medium of the future.

Her journey took her from very basic websites with just CSS and Javascript to Rails sites to Backbone apps and eventually opened up doors to building games, toys, API driven apps, and more.

Every day, she wrote a little note about what she did, and released everything publicly on GitHub.

It's one of the more inspiring approaches to self-learning we've seen. Definitely take some time and check it out.
